iAzeem: Empowering Inclusivity through Technology

iAzeem app conceptualized to primarily focus on a service-centralized accessibility-oriented app for the People Of Determination; subsumed by designing a graphical and interactive environment for learning miscellaneous informative content and access to generally appropriate information, incorporating official government recommendations and public facilities for the people of determination. The app will be designed to make use of AI and machine learning algorithms to assist application navigation and interaction to aid in speech and physical impediments.

CNN to detect Early Signs of Lung Cancer

Proposed a solution using Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) which utilizes sophisticated image classification method to promptly identify early indicators of lung cancer in medical images. CNN architecture: three convolutional layers: 32,64 and 128 filters; Max Pooling Layer; ReLUactivation functions; Dropout and batch normalization

Published Research Paper

In this research paper, I explored the effectiveness of two machine learning classifiers, Support Vector Classifier (SVC) and Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD), in detecting hate speech on Twitter. Using a publicly available dataset from Analytics Vidhya on Kaggle.com, which includes 32,000 tweets labeled as sexist/racist ('1') or not ('0'), I trained and evaluated the models. The primary objective was to compare the accuracy of these classifiers to determine their effectiveness in identifying hate speech.
